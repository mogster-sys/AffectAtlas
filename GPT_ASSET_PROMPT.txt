AFFECTATLAS -- DIGITAL ASSET GENERATION BRIEF
================================================

You are generating the complete digital asset suite
for AffectAtlas, a mobile and web app that transforms
emotions into complete design systems. The user
selects an emotion on Plutchik's Wheel of Emotions
and the entire interface transforms -- colors,
typography, spacing, motion, shadows, depth, shapes,
layout, iconography -- to EMBODY that emotion. It
then provides a deep design advisory with
research-backed guidance and exports production-ready
design tokens in 9 formats.

It is a design advisory tool for designers,
therapists, brand strategists, and anyone who works
with emotional communication.

The visual identity centers on Plutchik's Wheel: a
flower-shaped diagram of 8 primary emotions arranged
in opposing pairs, each with 3 intensity levels
(mild outer ring, moderate middle, intense center).
The wheel is the app's core interaction -- users tap
or rotate to select emotions.


THE PLUTCHIK WHEEL (reference for all assets)
----------------------------------------------
8 primary emotions in opposing pairs, each with a
signature color:

  Joy ........... #FFD700 (warm yellow-gold)
  Trust ......... #8BC34A (olive green)
  Fear .......... #9C27B0 (deep violet)
  Surprise ...... #03A9F4 (sky blue)
  Sadness ....... #5C6BC0 (blue-indigo)
  Disgust ....... #795548 (muddy brown-green)
  Anger ......... #F44336 (hot red)
  Anticipation .. #FF9800 (warm orange)

8 dyad emotions sit between the primaries:
  Love (Joy+Trust), Submission (Trust+Fear),
  Alarm (Fear+Surprise), Disappointment
  (Surprise+Sadness), Remorse (Sadness+Disgust),
  Contempt (Disgust+Anger), Aggressiveness
  (Anger+Anticipation), Optimism
  (Anticipation+Joy)

The wheel shape: concentric petal-like segments
radiating from center, each primary occupying a
45-degree arc. Intense emotions are at the center
(smaller, saturated), mild emotions at the outer
edge (larger, pastel). The overall shape resembles
an 8-petaled flower.

PLUS 13 extra "deepened" emotions that orbit the
wheel as contextual extensions: Awe, Frustration,
Contentment, Pride, Guilt, Inspiration,
Embarrassment, Depression, Loneliness, Jealousy,
Fright, Appalled, Confusion.

TOTAL: 29 emotions, each with 3 intensity levels,
each with a unique design system and deep advisory.


WHAT THE APP ACTUALLY DOES (critical for accuracy)
---------------------------------------------------
When a user selects an emotion, they get:

1. LIVE PREVIEW -- The entire screen transforms to
   embody that emotion. Joy becomes warm gold with
   bouncy rounded shapes. Fear becomes clinical
   violet with sharp edges. Each of the 29 emotions
   has a completely unique visual identity designed
   by Stitch (an AI design tool), including:
   - Unique headline + body font pairing
   - Full color palette (7 semantic + 5 palette)
   - Shape language (corner radii, corner style)
   - Motion character (duration, easing, feel)
   - Depth approach (shadows, glows, layers)
   - Layout density and alignment
   - Iconography style and weight

2. DEEP ADVISORY -- A scrollable panel with 11+
   sections of design guidance per emotion:
   - Hero section with North Star concept
   - Philosophy and rationale
   - Color tokens with mildToIntense spectrum
   - Typography specimens at multiple scale levels
   - Spacing scale with density guidance
   - Motion with bezier curve visualization
   - Depth/shadow approach with live examples
   - Shape/corner radius recommendations
   - Layout alignment and density rules
   - Iconography with Material Symbols examples
   - Do's and Don'ts rules
   - Thesaurus word cloud
   - Emotional context (how this emotion relates
     to and differs from similar emotions)

3. VARIANT ADVISORIES -- Some emotions have bonus
   variant screens. Love has 3 variants:
   - Love (base): warm rose, candlelit intimacy
   - Passion: deep wine, smoldering hearth
   - Lust: neon violet, electric midnight
   Users switch between variants via chip tabs at
   the top of the advisory panel.

4. EXPORT PANEL -- 9 production-ready export
   formats, selectable via horizontally scrollable
   tabs:
   - CSS Custom Properties
   - JSON (raw design system)
   - Swift (iOS UIKit/SwiftUI)
   - Kotlin (Jetpack Compose)
   - AI Prompt (natural language design brief)
   - Figma (Tokens Studio plugin format)
   - Tailwind (theme extend config)
   - SCSS (variables)
   - W3C Design Tokens (DTCG standard)

Each export includes the full design system:
colors, typography, shapes, spacing, motion, and
design guidance where available.

1. APP ICON (1024x1024, required for stores)
-----------------------------------------------
Design a square app icon at 1024x1024 pixels.

Concept: A simplified, stylized Plutchik wheel on
a dark background (#0A0A0B or very dark charcoal).
The wheel should be abstracted -- not a literal
diagram but an artistic interpretation. Think of
8 gradient petals radiating from center, each in
its emotion's color, with soft glow/bloom effects
where colors meet. The center could have a subtle
"A" letterform or simply be the bright convergence
point of all 8 colors.

Requirements:
- Must read clearly at 48x48px (phone home screen)
- No text in the icon
- The 8 Plutchik colors should all be present
- Dark background with the wheel as glowing element
- Avoid flat/clipart style -- this should feel like
  a premium design tool

  design to the full square with important content
  away from corners

  not flat fills. Think light emitting from within.

Variations needed:
- Standard (dark bg, colored wheel)
- Monochrome (for system UI contexts)
- Light background variant (for light mode contexts)

STYLE GUIDELINES FOR ALL ASSETS
=================================
- Dark backgrounds (#0A0A0B or near-black)
- The Plutchik wheel is always the chromatic hero
- Prefer glow/bloom effects over flat color fills
- Typography: geometric sans-serifs (Inter, Sora,
  or similar) for marketing materials
- Avoid clipart, cartoons, or emoji-style imagery
- Premium, editorial, design-tool aesthetic
- Think: if Figma and a psychology textbook had
  a beautiful, emotionally intelligent child
- The wheel should always feel like it could be
  rotating -- suggest motion even in stills
- Color should feel like it emerges from darkness,
  not placed on top of it
- No emojis anywhere in generated assets


COMMON GPT MISTAKES TO AVOID
==============================
These are errors from previous generation attempts.
Do NOT repeat them:

- Do not render the wheel as a flat pie chart or
  segmented circle. It is a FLOWER with PETALS --
  organic, overlapping, luminous.
- Do not use generic stock-photo phone mockups with
  random UI. The app has a specific look: dark bg,
  glowing wheel at bottom, preview area above.
- Do not make the icon too detailed. At 48px it
  must still read as "glowing flower shape."
- Do not use bright white backgrounds anywhere.
  The brand is DARK. Even light-mode variants
  should use warm off-whites, not pure #FFFFFF.
- Do not add drop shadows to the wheel. It GLOWS.
  It emits light. Shadows darken; this wheel
  illuminates.
- Do not use generic sans-serif for emotion
  examples. Each emotion has a SPECIFIC font pair
  listed above. Joy is Plus Jakarta Sans, not Arial.
- Do not show only 4-5 emotions. The app has 29.
  When showing breadth, show at least 6-8 distinct
  emotion states.
- Do not make screenshots look like wireframes or
  flat mockups. They should look like actual
  rendered app screens with real content.
- The export panel has 9 tabs, not 3. Do not show
  only "CSS, JSON, Swift" -- show the full
  scrollable tab strip.
