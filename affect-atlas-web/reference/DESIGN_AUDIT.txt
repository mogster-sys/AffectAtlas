pefter getfhAffectAtlas Design System Audit
================================
March 2026 -- Pre-Release Quality Review

This report compares DESIGN GUIDANCE (what the app promises)
against ACTUAL IMPLEMENTATION (what the code delivers) for
every emotion. Critical gaps that would be noticed by a
designer audience are flagged.


================================================================
KEY FINDINGS SUMMARY
================================================================

CRITICAL FONT MISMATCHES: 12 of 16 emotions have headline
fonts in designTokens.ts that do NOT match the Stitch prompts
or design guidance.

MISSING EMOTIONS: 13 expanded emotions (Awe, Confusion,
Depression, Loneliness, Guilt, Appalled, Frustration,
Jealousy, Inspiration, Contentment, Embarrassment, Fright,
Pride) have Stitch prompts and design guidance but are NOT
in designTokens.ts. They fall back to their parent primary
emotion's theme. This means 13 of 29 emotions render with
the WRONG design system.

INTENSITY FLATTENING: Several emotions (Anger, Anticipation)
show identical or near-identical tokens across all 3 intensity
levels. The mild/moderate/intense progression is not visible.

SHADOW SYSTEM: advisoryPanel.ts implements shadow rendering
per-emotion, but the depth styles are advisory-level data,
not theme-level. The actual app preview may not apply these.

MOTION: Motion characters are well-implemented in
advisoryPanel.ts (31 distinct transforms). This is a strength.

EMOTION-BY-EMOTION CRITICAL AUDIT
================================================================


1. JOY
------
Guidance:  Warm golden-yellow (#FFD700), bouncy rounded forms,
           Nunito or Quicksand bold, 24px corners, spring motion
Stitch:    Plus Jakarta Sans headline, Be Vietnam Pro body,
           16-32px corners, 350ms spring bounce
Tokens:    Plus Jakarta Sans headline, Be Vietnam Pro body,
           16/32/48px corners, rounded style
           Colors: #fdf6df bg, #7b5400 primary (moderate)

VERDICT: MOSTLY ALIGNED
+ Font pairing matches Stitch (not original guidance Nunito,
  but Stitch overrode that and tokens match Stitch)
+ Corner radii aligned (16px base, rounded style)
+ Warm palette aligned
- Joy:mild and Joy:intense use nearly identical values to
  moderate. The "Diffused Dawn" (mild) vs "Radiant Festival"
  (intense) distinction is not visible in the tokens.
- No spring bounce easing in tokens (motion is in advisory
  data only, not theme tokens)


2. TRUST
--------
Guidance:  Cool teal-blue (#1A3A4A), Newsreader serif,
           4-8px corners, restrained motion, monochromatic
Stitch:    Newsreader headline, Inter body, 4-8px smooth
           corners, 250ms restrained ease
Tokens:    Newsreader headline (mild/moderate), BUT
           Trust:intense uses Inter/Inter -- loses the serif

VERDICT: GOOD BUT INTENSE VARIANT BREAKS
+ Mild and moderate match perfectly
- Trust:intense drops Newsreader for Inter/Inter. The
  design guidance for intense Trust ("Maximum Institutional")
  calls for STRONGER institutional serif, not abandoning it.
  This is backwards -- the font should get MORE serif at
  intense, not less.
- All three intensities have nearly identical color palettes.
  The progression from "calm professional" to "maximum
  institutional" is not visible in color.


3. FEAR
-------
Guidance:  Dark violet/black (#0D0D0D), Epilogue thin (200
           weight), 0-4px sharp corners, compressed line-height,
           vignette inset shadows, tense motion
Stitch:    Epilogue headline, Inter body, 0-4px sharp, 200ms
           ease-in, cold violet palette
Tokens:    Epilogue headline (mild/moderate), BUT
           Fear:intense uses Inter/Inter -- loses Epilogue

VERDICT: GOOD BUT INTENSE VARIANT BREAKS (same issue as Trust)
+ Mild/moderate match well: Epilogue, dark bg, sharp corners
+ Corner style progresses: smooth(mild) -> smooth(mod) ->
  sharp(intense). Good intensity progression on shapes.
- Fear:intense drops Epilogue for Inter/Inter. The guidance
  says intense Fear should use even THINNER weights (200),
  not switch to a different font entirely. Epilogue at
  ultra-thin IS Fear. Losing it at intense -- the moment
  it matters most -- is the biggest miss.
- The "vignette" effect (darkness pressing inward from edges)
  described in guidance is not implementable in the current
  theme token structure.


4. SURPRISE
-----------
Guidance:  Neon magenta/cyan on deep indigo, Space Grotesk,
           MIXED corner radii (0px to 32px deliberate
           inconsistency), snappy overshoot motion, electric
Stitch:    Space Grotesk headline, Manrope body, 4px smooth
           (mild), then mixed, then 16/32/48 rounded (intense)
Tokens:    Space Grotesk headline, Plus Jakarta Sans body
           (mild), Manrope body (mod/intense)
           Corners: 4px smooth (mild), 4px smooth (mod),
           16/32/48 rounded (intense)

VERDICT: PARTIALLY ALIGNED
+ Space Grotesk headline is correct throughout
+ Dark theme on moderate/intense matches the deep indigo feel
- Body font inconsistency: mild uses Plus Jakarta Sans but
  should use Manrope per Stitch
- Surprise:moderate has smooth corners (4px) when the design
  guidance specifically calls for MIXED radii as a key
  feature. The deliberate inconsistency IS the surprise.
  Using uniform smooth corners defeats the concept.
- The neon magenta (#ff7cf5) and cyan (#50e1f9) are present
  but may not create enough "electric disruption" against
  the dark background without glow effects


5. SADNESS
----------
Guidance:  Pale desaturated blue-gray, Cormorant Garamond
           light weight, 4-8px smooth corners, slow 600ms
           drift motion, minimal shadows
Stitch:    Cormorant Garamond headline, Newsreader body,
           2-4px smooth, 600ms slow drift
Tokens:    Newsreader headline (mild/moderate), Cormorant
           Garamond headline (intense only)
           Newsreader or Public Sans body

VERDICT: FONT MISMATCH ON MILD/MODERATE
- Stitch specifies Cormorant Garamond as the HEADLINE font
  for Sadness. Tokens use Newsreader for mild/moderate and
  only switch to Cormorant Garamond at intense. This is
  backwards -- Cormorant Garamond's thin, contemplative
  serifs ARE sadness. Newsreader is Trust's font.
+ Colors are appropriately desaturated and cool
+ Light theme with pale blue-gray backgrounds matches
+ Corner radii (2-4px smooth) align with guidance
- Body font varies: Public Sans (mild) vs Newsreader
  (moderate/intense). Should be consistent.


6. DISGUST
----------
Guidance:  Sickly yellow-green, murky olive-brown, warm
           olive-black background (#141408), Syne headline,
           Work Sans body, 6px organic corners, 500ms oozing
           motion, spreading olive-tinted shadows
Stitch:    Syne headline, Work Sans body, 4-12px organic,
           500ms cubic-bezier(0.4, 0, 0.6, 1)
Tokens:    Epilogue headline (mild), Epilogue/Work Sans
           (moderate/intense)
           Colors: #141405/#141407 bg -- close to guidance
           Corners: 2-4px smooth

VERDICT: CRITICAL FONT MISMATCH
- Guidance and Stitch both specify SYNE as headline font.
  Tokens use EPILOGUE. These are completely different fonts.
  Syne has organic irregularity that embodies contamination.
  Epilogue is geometric and clinical -- that's Fear's font.
  Using Fear's font for Disgust is a category error.
+ Background colors are close (#141405 vs #141408)
+ Dark theme is correct
- Corner radii too small (2-4px vs guidance 6-12px). Disgust
  should be organic/soft, not clinical/sharp.
- cornerStyle is "smooth" but should feel more "organic"


7. ANGER
--------
Guidance:  Saturated crimson on near-black, Oswald black
           (condensed uppercase), 0px razor sharp corners,
           150ms explosive snap, heavy crushing shadows
Stitch:    Oswald headline, Inter body, 0px sharp, 150ms
           cubic-bezier(0.2, 0, 0, 1)
Tokens:    Inter headline, Inter body (ALL intensities)
           Corners: 0px sharp -- correct
           Colors: #200604 bg, #ff8e80 primary -- warm but
           not the saturated crimson of guidance

VERDICT: CRITICAL FONT MISMATCH
- Guidance and Stitch both specify OSWALD (condensed
  uppercase, heavy weight) as Anger's headline font. Tokens
  use INTER for everything. Inter is a neutral humanist
  sans-serif -- it has zero emotional weight. It's literally
  the most neutral font in the Google Fonts library.
  Using Inter for Anger is like whispering when you should
  be screaming.
+ 0px sharp corners are correct
+ Dark theme is correct
- All three intensity levels (mild/moderate/intense) have
  nearly identical tokens. The progression from "Contained
  Tension" to "Controlled Combustion" to "Total Eruption"
  is invisible in the rendered output.
- Primary color #ff8e80 is a SALMON PINK, not crimson.
  The guidance calls for #930012 (deep crimson) and #ff0000.
  Salmon pink is not angry.

================================================================
CROSS-CUTTING ISSUES
================================================================

1. FONT DIVERSITY
   The design guidance specifies 16+ distinct headline fonts
   across all emotions. The actual tokens use primarily:
   - Plus Jakarta Sans (Joy, Anticipation, Disappointment,
     Optimism) -- 4 emotions sharing one font
   - Inter (Anger, Submission, Trust:intense, Fear:intense)
     -- 4+ emotions sharing one neutral font
   - Newsreader (Trust, Sadness:mild/mod, Contempt)
   - Space Grotesk (Surprise, Alarm, Aggressiveness)
   - Epilogue (Fear, Disgust)
   - Noto Serif (Love, Remorse)
   - Cormorant Garamond (Sadness:intense only)

   MISSING from tokens entirely:
   - Oswald (Anger, Alarm, Fright) -- the heavy condensed voice
   - Syne (Disgust) -- the organic irregular voice
   - Urbanist (Anticipation) -- the directed narrow voice
   - Crimson Pro (Love) -- the intimate warm voice
   - Maven Pro (Submission) -- the soft yielding voice
   - Bodoni Moda (Contempt, Jealousy) -- the aristocratic voice
   - Spectral (Guilt) -- the literary confessional voice
   - Cormorant (Awe, Sadness) -- the monumental voice
   - Playfair Display (Pride) -- the ceremonial voice
   - Chakra Petch (Appalled) -- the angular uncomfortable voice

   This is the single biggest gap. The font IS the emotion
   in this system. Showing Inter for Anger or Plus Jakarta
   Sans for Anticipation is like showing blue for Anger or
   red for Sadness -- it contradicts the message.

2. INTENSITY FLATTENING
   Several emotions show no meaningful progression across
   intensity levels:
   - Anger: identical tokens for all 3 intensities
   - Anticipation: moderate and intense are identical
   - Aggressiveness: very similar across all 3
   - Joy: minor color shifts only

   The guidance describes dramatically different experiences
   at each level (e.g. Joy: "Diffused Dawn" vs "Radiant
   Festival"). The tokens don't deliver this.

3. EXPANDED EMOTION COVERAGE
   13 of 29 emotions have no dedicated theme tokens. For a
   product that promises to map EVERY emotion to a unique
   design system, having 45% of emotions fall back to a
   parent's theme is a significant gap. The most damaging
   fallbacks are:
   - Embarrassment -> Fear (warm -> cold)
   - Pride -> Anger (ceremonial -> aggressive)
   - Jealousy -> Anticipation (toxic green -> warm amber)
   - Guilt -> Disgust (moral weight -> biological revulsion)
   - Awe -> Surprise (vast contemplation -> electric shock)

4. ADVISORY PANEL vs PREVIEW
   The advisoryPanel.ts is where the design system is
   EXPLAINED (typography specimens, color palettes, spacing
   bars, motion demos). This is well-implemented with 11
   detailed sections. But the PREVIEW of what a screen
   would look like using these tokens may not apply the
   full guidance (shadows, spacing density, layout alignment).

5. SHADOW/DEPTH SYSTEM
   Each emotion has unique shadow approaches in the advisory
   data (warm brown for Joy, vignette for Fear, spreading
   olive for Disgust, crushing black for Anger, luminous
   glow for Awe). These are described in the advisory but
   not stored as reusable theme tokens.


================================================================
RECOMMENDATIONS (Priority Order)
================================================================

1. FIX HEADLINE FONTS (Critical)
   Replace the generic fonts in designTokens.ts with the
   Stitch-specified fonts. This is the highest-impact change:
   - Anger: Inter -> Oswald
   - Disgust: Epilogue -> Syne
   - Anticipation: Plus Jakarta Sans -> Urbanist
   - Love: Noto Serif -> Crimson Pro
   - Submission: Inter -> Maven Pro
   - Contempt: Newsreader -> Bodoni Moda
   - Aggressiveness: Space Grotesk -> Teko (or Impact/Anton)
   - Sadness: Newsreader -> Cormorant Garamond (all levels)
   - Alarm: Space Grotesk -> Oswald/Barlow Condensed

2. ADD EXPANDED EMOTION TOKENS (High)
   Create dedicated designTokens entries for the 13 expanded
   emotions, especially the severe mismatches:
   - Embarrassment, Pride, Jealousy, Guilt, Awe (all severe)
   - Confusion, Depression, Loneliness (moderate impact)

3. FIX INTENSITY PROGRESSION (Medium)
   Ensure each emotion shows meaningful visual differences
   across mild/moderate/intense, particularly:
   - Anger (currently identical across all 3)
   - Anticipation (moderate = intense)
   - Color saturation, font weight, spacing density, and
     corner radius should all shift with intensity

4. ADD BODY FONT DIFFERENTIATION (Medium)
   Many emotions use Inter or Plus Jakarta Sans as body font.
   The Stitch prompts specify distinct body fonts that
   complement the headline:
   - Fear: Inter (correct -- clinical contrast works)
   - Joy: Be Vietnam Pro (correct in some variants)
   - Disgust: Work Sans (not Epilogue)
   - Sadness: Newsreader body (double-serif pairing)

5. VERIFY COLOR ACCURACY (Low-Medium)
   Some primary colors don't match the emotional intent:
   - Anger primary #ff8e80 is salmon pink, not crimson
   - Compare all palette colors against Stitch screenshots


================================================================
WHAT'S WORKING WELL
================================================================

- Motion system: 31 distinct motion characters with unique
  transforms, well-mapped to emotions
- Dark/light theme selection: appropriate per emotion
- Corner style vocabulary: sharp/smooth/rounded correctly
  mapped to emotional families
- Advisory panel structure: 11 comprehensive sections that
  explain the design system clearly
- Color science: OKLCH color space for perceptually uniform
  palettes is technically sound
- The 8 primary + 8 dyad structure covers Plutchik's model
- Spacing scales exist per emotion in advisory data
