Anticipation -- Perplexity Design Research
===========================================
Source: Perplexity AI research, early 2026


VISUAL DESIGN SYSTEM AESTHETICS FOR ANTICIPATION
==================================================

Anticipation in visual design is expressed through open-ended,
directional, and slightly unresolved aesthetics: movement cues,
incompleteness, and subtle tension rather than closure.

Core visual qualities
Directionality and guidance: Arrows, leading lines, progressive
disclosure, and "next-step" affordances signal that something is
about to happen or be revealed.

Incompleteness: Cropped elements, partial reveals behind masks or
frosted panels, and content that fades off-screen leave users
expecting more.

Temporal cues: Progress indicators, staged animations, and
microinteractions that hint at an upcoming state (e.g., a button
that subtly "charges" on hover) imply imminent change.

Light tension, not comfort: Slight asymmetry, off-center focal
points, and controlled whitespace create a feeling of "about to
resolve" rather than calm equilibrium.


COLOR AND TONE
--------------
High-energy but not urgent: Warm but softened hues (yellow-orange,
coral, soft magenta) read as energetic and optimistic without the
alarm of pure red.

Gradients and transitions: Gradients, especially from cool to warm
or dark to light, visually encode movement from "now" to "soon."

Contrast for focus: Using contrast on interactive or future-facing
elements (next step, upcoming feature, CTA) draws the eye toward
what's coming rather than what's current.


MOTION AND MICROINTERACTIONS
-----------------------------
Easing that overshoots or delays: Slight anticipation curves (a
small backward motion or scale-down before expanding) mirror
physical anticipation and are common in emotional UX.

Sequential reveal: Staggered animations, step-by-step transitions,
and storytelling sequences build a narrative that users expect to
continue.

Responsive hints: Hover states, subtle pulsing, or shimmer effects
indicate latent potential -- something will happen if the user
commits.


COMPOSITION AND LAYOUT
-----------------------
Narrative flow: Layouts that clearly read left-to-right or top-to-
bottom as a story (hero -> teaser -> teaser continuation) keep
attention flowing forward.

Visible but locked content: Teasers, blurred previews, and "next
chapter" tiles that are visible but gated naturally evoke
anticipation.

Hierarchy toward the future: Emphasis on upcoming actions or
timelines (e.g., "next match," "tomorrow's tasks") through size
and placement shifts emotional weight to the future.


MAPPING INTO A DESIGN SYSTEM
------------------------------
A "future/next" color role (e.g., accent gradient or optimistic
warm hue) used consistently for upcoming states.

A motion token set with anticipation-like easing, delays, and
stagger patterns for reveals and state changes.

Pattern primitives: partial cards, teaser modules, progress
indicators, and arrows/chevrons as standard components guiding
toward "what's next."


MOBILE APP TOKENS FOR ANTICIPATION
====================================

Token naming strategy
Use semantic, stateful names: color.accent.next, motion.reveal.
anticipation, opacity.surface.teaser. Keep platform-neutral.

Color tokens
color.accent.next -- primary "future/next" accent (soft orange/coral)
color.accent.next.hover -- slightly darker or more saturated
color.bg.teaser -- subtle tinted background for upcoming content cards
color.border.future -- used on "next step" buttons, timeline items
color.text.future -- for labels like "Up next," "Tomorrow"

Usage: color.accent.next only for elements that advance state or
reveal something. Pair color.bg.teaser with reduced opacity imagery.

Motion tokens
motion.reveal.anticipation -- Duration 220-260ms, ease-in then
ease-out (anticipation-like: quick scale down 0.96 -> 1.0, or
small backward translation -> forward)

motion.hover.future -- Duration 120-160ms, ease-out

motion.progress.nextStep -- Duration 400-800ms loops, linear for
looping progress, expressive ease for step change

Usage: motion.reveal.anticipation on components that appear as
user navigates forward. Expressive curves only for "forward" or
"reveal" transitions; back navigation stays neutral.

Layout/elevation/opacity tokens
elevation.card.teaser -- slightly higher than normal content
radius.card.future -- marginally more rounded for "future" tiles
opacity.surface.teaser -- 0.7-0.85 for upcoming items
opacity.scrim.futureOverlay -- 0.1-0.2 for "coming soon" states

State tokens for "next"
state.current -- active, in-use items
state.next -- soon-to-be-active (upcoming, next level, tomorrow)
state.lockedFuture -- visible but inaccessible (coming soon)

Each state binds to specific visual tokens: state.next uses
color.accent.next + motion.reveal.anticipation + elevation.card.teaser


WEB UI COMPONENT PATTERNS FOR ANTICIPATION
============================================

Next-step CTA pattern
A button or bar that clearly points to the most likely next action.
Primary "Next" CTA at end of flows with copy naming the upcoming
state. Directional affordances: right-chevrons, forward arrows,
steppers showing current + highlighted "next" step.

Key behavior: animate with brief slide/scale-in (translateY 6px -> 0,
scale 0.96 -> 1, ~220ms ease-in-out). On hover, subtle forward nudge.

Teaser card for upcoming content
Cards showing just enough about something coming next. "Up next,"
"Coming soon," "Tomorrow" badges. Visually differentiated: lighter
background tint, subtle lock/time icon for not-yet-available. Optional
partial blur/scrim over imagery for incomplete access.

Behavior: on scroll into view, anticipation reveal motion (small
offset + opacity 0 -> 1). On hover, raise elevation and reveal one
extra metadata line.

Progressive disclosure "peek" pattern
Start with concise summary + visible "Show more." On expand, animate
height/opacity and rotate icon. Content slides in staggered (titles
first, then details). Accent underline or arrow animates on hover
like it's "pulling" into next layer.

Predictive suggestion panel
Pre-offering likely next actions. "Next best actions" strip with 2-4
buttons. Appears after main action resolves with soft slide/fade.
Secondary styling so it feels like a helpful nudge.

Anticipatory stepper/timeline
Emphasize the next step (not all future steps): slightly brighter,
pulsing dot or glow. Hover future steps to see preview tooltip.


EMOTION WHEEL INTEGRATION NOTES
=================================

Anticipation as a semantic layer: when a designer picks "anticipation"
from the emotion wheel, show a gallery with next-step CTAs, teaser
cards, progressive disclosure, and predictive panels already skinned
in anticipation colors and motions.

Interpolation: emotions near each other on the wheel can blend tokens.
Anticipation + Joy -> optimistic playful future state.
Intensity (distance from center) controls saturation and motion energy.

One canonical "emotion definition" per wheel segment. Visual
consistency from WebGL mood to Figma preview to React/Tailwind UI.
