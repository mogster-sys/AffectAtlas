Joy / Happy — Perplexity Design Research
=========================================
Source: Perplexity AI research, early 2026


VISUAL DESIGN SYSTEM AESTHETICS FOR JOY
========================================

Happy emotions in visual systems are usually expressed through
bright warm colors, rounded friendly forms, playful motion,
and light, open layouts.


COLOR
-----
Use warm hues: saturated yellows, oranges, and warm reds tend
to signal joy, enthusiasm, and playfulness.

Add brightness and contrast: lighter tints and higher brightness
feel more uplifting than dark, muted palettes.

Use accents, not overload: small bursts of warm color on a neutral
base keep happiness feeling energetic rather than chaotic.


SHAPE AND ICONOGRAPHY
---------------------
Favor rounded shapes: circles, soft pills, and curves are strongly
associated with positive, non-threatening feelings and "bubbly" fun.

Avoid sharp angles for happy moments: hard, jagged shapes
subconsciously hint at danger or tension, which can undercut joy.

Use simple, recognizable icons: clear, friendly metaphors (stars,
suns, confetti, hearts) support feelings of delight and celebration.


TYPOGRAPHY
----------
Choose humanist or rounded sans-serifs: softer terminals and
generous curves feel more approachable and cheerful than rigid,
condensed faces.

Use light to medium weights with larger sizes: airy, open type
with comfortable spacing feels relaxed and optimistic.

Add small expressive touches: occasional playful display fonts
or emphasized words can create moments of joy when used sparingly.


LAYOUT, MOTION, AND IMAGERY
----------------------------
Keep layouts spacious and clear: intuitive hierarchy and breathing
room support a pleasurable, low-friction experience that users
associate with positive feelings.

Use micro-animations for delight: small celebratory motions on
success states (checkmarks popping, confetti bursts) reliably
evoke happiness and accomplishment.

Select joyful imagery: photos or illustrations with smiling faces,
upward curves, natural light, and slightly exaggerated, vibrant
palettes reinforce a happy tone.


QUICK "HAPPY" SYSTEM RECIPE
----------------------------
Palette: bright yellow + warm coral accent on a soft neutral base.
Shapes: circular buttons, pill-shaped chips, rounded cards, curved dividers.
Type: rounded sans-serif for UI, playful display font for key celebratory moments.
Motion: subtle bounces and easing-out transitions on positive actions and success screens.


COLOR PALETTES THAT EVOKE JOY
==============================

Principles for "Happy" Palettes
- Use warm highs: saturated yellows and oranges are strongly
  associated with joy, optimism, and fun.
- Add fresh cools: greens and blue-greens support feelings of
  relaxed, comfortable happiness and balance warmth.
- Keep it light: tints, pastels, and off-whites feel airy and
  uplifting, while heavy darks can dampen the mood.

Example High-Energy Palettes
- Sunny Confetti: bright yellow (#FFD447), tangerine (#FF8A3D),
  teal (#44D9E6), warm off-white (#FFF8E8).
  Works for celebratory states, onboarding success, gamified UI.
- Bubblegum Arcade: candy pink (#FF5D9E), light magenta (#FF8AD8),
  lemon yellow (#FFD447), aqua (#44D9E6).
  Ideal for youth-focused, kawaii, or nostalgic interfaces.

Example Soft-Happy Palettes
- Lemon Sherbet: soft lemon (#FFF7AE), peach (#FFE0C2),
  blush (#FFD1E3), baby blue (#CCEAF5).
  Good for productivity, lifestyle, or education apps.
- Soft Skies: misty lilac (#F9F5FF), light sky blue (#D7F3FF),
  minty aqua (#C4F8FF), cotton pink white (#FFEEF2).
  Suits minimal UIs where gentle joy and clarity matter.


SHAPES THAT COMMUNICATE JOY
============================

Core Friendly Shapes
- Circles and ovals: signal warmth, unity, community, friendliness;
  no threatening angles; consistently rated as pleasant and "good."
- Rounded rectangles/pills: combine stability with softness,
  making buttons and cards feel approachable and safe.
- Hearts, spirals, sun-like forms: associated with love, growth,
  and playful happiness.

Curves vs Angles
- Curved lines and organic blobs: comfort, safety, reduced stress,
  positive affect.
- Sharp angles (V-shapes, spikes): alertness, caution, threat —
  undermine joyful tone if overused.


YELLOW USAGE WITHOUT EYE STRAIN
================================

Choose the right yellow
- Prefer muted or light yellows (butter, cream, honey) for large
  areas; save intense canary/neon for small highlights.
- Shift toward amber/mustard for better readability and less glare,
  especially on mobile.

Manage contrast and placement
- Pair yellow with dark text (charcoal, navy, deep purple) and
  verify at least 4.5:1 contrast for normal text (WCAG).
- Avoid yellow text on light backgrounds.
- Limit large full-screen yellow backgrounds; use strips, cards,
  CTAs, or gradients so the eye can rest.

Layout and theming
- On yellow backgrounds, choose heavier weights and larger sizes
  with clean sans-serifs to improve legibility.
- Provide darker or reduced-brightness theme where possible.

ONBOARDING PALETTE GUIDELINES
==============================

60-30-10 Rule
- Base (60%): very light warm neutrals — cream, soft peach,
  warm gray — for backgrounds.
- Primary (30%): one warm happy color (golden yellow #FFC936,
  tangerine #FF8A3D, peach #FFD9C2) for illustrations and UI blocks.
- Accent (10%): 1-2 brighter hues (coral, pink, aqua) for CTAs
  and highlights.

Example Onboarding Palette
- Backgrounds: #FFF7EC (warm cream), #FFEDE2 (very light peach)
- Primary: #FFB74D (warm orange), #FFE65A (sunny yellow)
- Accents: #FF6F61 (coral), #3BB9FF (sky blue)
- Text: #2B2B33 (charcoal)

Balance delight with legibility
- Reserve strongest yellow/orange for primary actions and highlights.
- Keep body text in dark neutrals; let illustrations carry cheer.
- Test in both light and dark modes; tone saturation down if
  first impression feels "shouty" on mobile.
