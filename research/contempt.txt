Contempt -- Perplexity Design Research
========================================
Source: Perplexity AI research, early 2026


VISUAL DESIGN SYSTEM AESTHETICS FOR CONTEMPT
==============================================

Contempt in visual design is usually conveyed through "cold
superiority": sharp, distancing, and slightly mocking aesthetics
rather than outright aggression or chaos.

Core emotional profile
Contempt = a rejection emotion: you judge something as inferior
and distance yourself from it rather than engage or fix it.

It tends to feel certain, cold, and externally blaming ("you are
beneath me"), not confused or self-blaming.


COLOR CUES
-----------

Desaturated, cold palettes: greys, dirty whites, cold blues, and
black often signal negative, distancing emotions.

Accents of toxic green or dull yellow can echo disgust/disdain
without the intensity of pure red anger.

Low overall saturation with a few hard, high-contrast accents
(e.g., black + a single harsh color) can feel cutting and
judgmental.


SHAPE, LINE, AND COMPOSITION
------------------------------

Angular, downward, or "pointing down" shapes communicate negative,
hostile or rejecting emotion; downward-pointing triangles are
frequently used to depict negative states like hate, reproach,
resentment, shame.

Sharp, thin, precise lines, tight spacing, and visually "boxed out"
regions reinforce exclusion and distance.

Asymmetry where one area is visually dominant and "above" another
(top-heavy layouts, elevated panels) can read as hierarchical and
patronizing.


TYPOGRAPHY AND MICRO-AESTHETICS
---------------------------------

Condensed, high-contrast serif or grotesque sans faces with sharp
terminals can feel cold and severe when paired with cool, dark
palettes.

Excess tracking, all-caps, and rigid alignment can imply emotional
distance and formality rather than warmth.

Avoid playful curvature or handwritten styles; these usually
communicate friendliness or vulnerability rather than disdain.


PRACTICAL DESIGN RECIPE FOR CONTEMPT
======================================

Palette: off-white + slate/black base, with a small accent of
acidic green or dirty yellow.

Forms: thin, downward-angled rules, triangles pointing down or
away, and elements visually "looking down on" others via vertical
placement.

Layout: lots of cold negative space around a dominant block,
pushing secondary elements low or small to feel "beneath" it.

Type: sharp, highly controlled typography (condensed, high-contrast,
or geometric), slightly loose letterspacing and minimal ornament,
no rounded corners.


COMPACT CONTEMPT SPEC
=======================

1. Emotion definition
Label: Contempt
Core appraisal: "You (or this group) are intrinsically inferior /
beneath me."
Direction: Other-focused, socially rejecting; not self-blaming.
Typical blend: Sits between anger (moral offense) and disgust
(moral/physical revulsion), but colder and more controlled.

2. Visual identity tokens

2.1 Color
Base hues: off-white / bone, cool grey (light and dark), cold
blue-grey.

Accent hues (sparingly): acidic green (hint of disgust), dirty
yellow (optional, for "smug" or "sour" tone).

System notes: low to medium saturation overall, high contrast
between light background and dark details. Avoid warm reds/oranges
(anger) and heavy browns (pure disgust) except in blends.

Token examples:
cont-01-bg: #ECEFF1
cont-02-ink: #1C1F24
cont-03-acid: #8BC34A (or sharper variant)
cont-04-muted: #90A4AE

2.2 Shape and layout
Primary shape: downward-pointing triangle (narrow, tall).
Secondary shapes: stacked downward chevrons, thin vertical
rectangles / pillars.

Line style: thin, precise, straight; almost no curvature.

Layout logic: top-heavy compositions (dominant element high,
secondary elements small and low). Asymmetry: weight biased to
one side to echo "one-sided lip raise." Ample cold negative space
around the dominant element.

Icon/graphic rules: default contempt glyph is tall, narrow
triangle pointing down, 1-2px outline in dark ink color.
Background motif: repeated, faint downward chevrons, low opacity.


MOTION AND INTERACTION
========================

Timing: slightly delayed responses (small beat before reacting)
to read as "unimpressed." Slow, smooth easing; avoid snappy,
bouncy animations.

Directionality: dismissive motions that move elements down or
away from the character/player. When entering a scene, character
may appear from a higher position and descend slowly.

UI overlays when contempt is active: cold tint pass (very subtle
blue-grey), thin angular frames or underlines near the speaking
character's nameplate/portrait. Optional micro-animation: a
narrow triangle icon briefly appears then fades.


CONTEMPT VS ANGER VS DISGUST
===============================

Contempt: cold, controlled, distancing. Feels certain and
superior. Visual: sharp, clean, asymmetric, cold.

Anger: hot, reactive, confrontational. Wants to change or
destroy. Visual: bold, dense, aggressive, warm/hot colors.

Disgust: visceral, recoiling, contamination-focused. Wants to
expel or avoid. Visual: organic distortion, sickly colors,
textural corruption.

Key distinction: contempt judges intrinsic worth ("you ARE
inferior"), anger judges actions ("you DID wrong"), disgust
judges contamination ("you are TAINTED").


CONTEXT AND USAGE NOTES
=========================

Narrative meaning: use contempt when the character judges another
as inherently lesser, not just wrong. Good for: aristocratic
rivals, elitist technocrats, moralistic inquisitors.

Avoid overuse: too much contempt can make a character irredeemably
alienating; reserve for key beats or clearly antagonistic roles.
For more nuance, mix small amounts into anger or disappointment
instead of running at 100%.
